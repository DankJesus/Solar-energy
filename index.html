Solar Energy

What is it?

Energy provided by the sun! Solar energy is both renewable and infinite (well, almost infinite)

Why is it renewable?

As the sun is a near-infinite energy, the energy it provides can be used for electricity as long as there is a sun.

Why aren't we using it then?

To begin, contructing solar panels to absorb the sun's energy costs a lot of money. A lot more than using fossil fuels.
It's also a good idea to note that solar energy only works when there is sun out.
Therefore, during the night or in places where there is little sun, very little-to-no solar energy is produced.

Why isn't solar energy infinite?

You'll be dead by the time the sun dies, but in about 7 billion years we won't have a sun. No sun, no solar energy. The Earth will have been engulfed in flames by then, anyway.

Do you think that we will ever resort to using solar energy?

Yes and no.
Fossil fuels are destined to run out soon, but other renewable resources such as hydroelectricity and wind mills can, and will, be used.
